# Background
The Skoonies are a very old and tribal gang in Whitecliff. While their Mythos makes them seem short and often has Sleepers mistake them for kids, they range from young to old. The gang seems disinterested in traditional organized crime pursuits, like drug and weapon trafficking, and its members are vagrants living on the streets and in the sewers (in fact, they originally awakened to their Mythos through one of the “Maws”, see The Undertrash). Unbeknownst to outsiders, they style themselves custodians of the concrete jungle and often cultivate and decorate alleys, underground passages, and hovels under bridges. Their criminal activity amounts to loitering, tagging, squatting, and littering until someone interferes with their urban landscaping. They then retaliate disproportionately, becoming violent and vindictive, holding grudges for years, and harrassing en masse whoever they see as a threat to their lifestyle. Locals in Whitecliff know to turn back when they see a Skoonie and just leave them alone, but the gang has been a troublesome nuisance here for decades.

The Errants are Cyrus/Beaumont’s newly formed gang, made of idealistic Whitecliff youngsters who want to change the face of the neighborhood and the City and subscribe to the Albion movement. They ride off-road bikes, sometimes in pairs, and dress like punks and outlaws in studded biker jackets. One of their most prominent features is their biker helmets, painted over with motifs of knightly helms and bearing stylized visors. Their current objective, as laid by Calvin Cyrus, is to grow their territory, laying the groundwork for the expansion of the Albion Enclave. The Errants are deeply loyal to their community, a loyalty that is only exceeded by their obedience to their king. They envision an Albion that can benefit all residents, but first all other malign influences must be removed – which is what brought them to clash with the Skoonies.

## THE FIGHT
The Skoonies are led tonight by Screaming Moon, their wiry and vicious elder, while the Errants act as a circle: they all abide by their objective independently without anyone having authority over the others. Both gangs want the same thing: crush their enemies, disband them, and prove they rule Whitecliff. Both gangs have a size factor of 2.

The battle will ensue shortly after the PCs arrive and will rapidly escalate to violence and blood. Don’t play out the actions of the NPC gangs against one another; instead, resolve it narratively. If the crew has a favorite, they’re losing, driving the crew to intervene. Otherwise, the gangs might both mark the crew as a third contender or simply unwanted attention and send some of their members to attack the crew. If the crew is only watching from afar, the Errants eventually subdue the Skoonies, but both sides suffer extensive injuries and several casualties.

The PCs have a momentary chance to stop the fight before it starts, but the Skoonies are suspicious-3 while the Errants are loyal-3 to their leader and cause, so Convincing both sides to stand down proves challenging. Once the fight begins, give all combatants too-enragedto-listen-4 if anyone tries to stop them from fighting. Calling Precinct 63 will reveal the police are reluctant-2 to intervene unless innocent residents are caught in the fray, and prefer to let the gangs do their dirty work by eliminating each other.
## AFTER THE FIGHT
The fight dies down when a clear victor emerges, or when there are too many wounded to continue. Whoever has the upper hand will shout at the other gang never to show their faces in Whitecliff, as the losers flee. The Skoonies will disperse back to their alleys, hovels, and sewer tunnels while the Errants return to their homes or to the junkyard. This may allow the PCs to exchange a few words with each gang

If the PCs sided with either gang in the fight, the other gang takes hates-your-guts-3 against them. If that rival gang is the Skoonies, this status is especially persistent and difficult to remove (Player’s Guide, page 226) as they tend to hold grudges for long.

If they haven’t shown up during the fight, the police and emergency medical services eventually arrive to treat and arrest injured gangsters and bag the bodies of the dead. They are followed by the media, whose coverage of the story could implicate the PCs and either or both gangs, while highlighting Mayor Ricci’s failure to confront crime head on. Calvin Cyrus may show up to interview for the news on behalf of the CCC.

# Clues
## TALKING TO THE SKOONIES
- The Skoonies are naturally tribal and reclusive, but if a PC gets a Skoonie to talk or follows them to their hiding place, they can learn more about the customs and organization of this gang (see above).
- The Skoonies’ beef with the Errants is that they plan to transform the urban landscape into something else. It’s already started at the community garden. They also don’t care for anyone encroaching on their space, let alone a rival gang
- The Skoonies have no problems with Mayor Ricci, in fact they hardly know her name. She and the police let them be and in return the Skoonies stand down when the police show up, for the most part.
## Talking to the errants
There is very little that the Errants are allowed to candidly share; Investigating beyond the following clues would trigger their **loyal-3** status.

However, the Errants are looking to recruit heroes; if the crew wants to join them, the gang will ask them to first prove themselves with a “quest”: “slay the dragon” in the [[overgrown ruin]] or help them recruit “the best knight of us all” at [[the junkyard]].
- The Errants come across as wayward punks, not hardened criminals. They think of themselves as freedom fighters and renegades. Most of them regret their violent actions but have been disillusioned with all other solutions. Some are even shaken after the fight.
- The Errants are on a “quest” (as they put it) to transform Whitecliff into a beautiful, verdant “kingdom” that is free and just, where people can be their legendary selves. Mayor Ricci, the police, and other gangs are just standing in their way by trying to maintain the status quo.
- Some of the Errants can be linked to the vandalizing of [[Double Dragon Chinese Restaurant|the Double Dragon restaurant]]. Their helmets and bikes match the descriptions of the perps, but they have alibis backed by various members of the community (all of whom volunteer at [[The Community Gardens|the community garden]]). One of them doesn’t stop sneezing; mystical inspection would reveal he’s under a curse (the work of Huáng Xiáxiá).
- Those who partook in trashing the Double Dragon say they feel bad about it, but that it was essential to reveal Mayor Ricci’s inability to keep the peace, that her weakness needs to be shown to all. They are sorry for the damages they caused, claiming that they chose well-off businesses who could endure it, for the good of all other businesses in the neighborhood.
- Beyond the Mist, the Errants are young medieval knights clad in armor, their baseball bats are shining lances, and their off-road bikes are gallant steeds. They are Touched by a Mythos whose Rift is hidden by writhing mist (Cyrus/ Beaumont) but which can also be linked to the Albion Enclave at [[The Community Gardens|the community garden]].
# Dangers
## The skoonies
The Skoonies (Pukwudgies) are men and women of all ages who live out on the streets and protect the concrete jungle as if they were its keepers. They appear as short, squabby, or haunched individuals, dressed in tattered simple clothes and carrying makeshift spears and bows. Strange spikes come out of their faces, as if they were little imps. Thanks to their Mythos, the Skoonies can disappear in broad daylight, cause accidents, and brew potent poisons that they apply to their weapons.

## THE ERRANTS
The Errants are a biker gang consisting mostly of young adults of varying genders. They dress in steel-studded biker garb, wear motorcycle helmets designed like knightly helms, and carry baseball bats as weapons with trash can covers as shields. Their power shines when they are fighting for what they perceive as a noble cause.

Use the Errant’s tags to weaken the PCs’ moves, based on the situation. As normal, these story tags cannot be invoked more than once in each exchange. For example:

**Offensive moves**: *strength-of-conviction* + *makeshift shield* (+ *two-wheeled steed* if the Errant is taking evasive maneuvers)

**Face Danger**: *strength-of-conviction* + *junk-made sword* (+ *two-wheeled steed* if the Errant is charging)
