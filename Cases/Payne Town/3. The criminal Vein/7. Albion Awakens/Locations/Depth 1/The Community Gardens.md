# Background
The community garden is the first part of Whitecliff to transform into the Albion Enclave. While outwardly it appears as a normal green patch of soil in the middle of an urban area, those who step in find that the rows of buildings on either of its sides are pushed far away, opening up a great tract of agricultural land in the middle of Whitecliff. The gate of the community garden is a thin place that leads into the Enclave but transitioning in and out of the Enclave is fluid, and in many places the Enclave overlaps mundane reality. The greater the power of the Errants and Cyrus/Beaumont grows, the more the Enclave spreads, and the more thin places show up all across the borough.

The Enclave exudes pastoral tranquility that the people of Whitecliff find relaxing and idyllic. They show up here in droves to till the land, sow, water, and reap. Given the legendary nature of the soil, and seeds provided by Céline Rowland (Lady of the Lake), their crops are plentiful and they produce food in steadily increasing amounts, enough to sustain the neighborhood, just as Beaumont has planned.

Those who work here are slowly changing their clothes and tools to those of medieval farmers, while nearby convenience stores and bodegas are gradually transforming into marketplaces where the produce is traded. Inside the Enclave, the buildings of Whitecliff appear to be made of stone and have thatched roofs. While Sleepers turn a blind eye to this, Rifts may wonder at this change.

The farmers here are candid about this positive change, unless they suspect someone is snooping around with the intention of interfering with their new hipster movement, in which case they will become **guarded-2**.

## HOPE AND GLORY
Albion perfuses everything that is good and wholesome in the Arthurian legends, filling everyone who spends time here with positive thoughts and feelings. There is an *ambient wholesomeness* here that supports healing, recovery, and healthy growth.

As the setting for the adventures of the Knights of the Round Table, the Enclave is also a hotbed for mysterious woodlands and grottos, wrongs that must be put right, and plenty of opportunities for undertaking noble quests. Unfortunately, it also attracts invaders to attack it, like the historical Saxons that became the foreign villains in some of Arthur’s legends.

# Clues
- Abandoned until recently, this community garden has been revived by the [[Council of Concerned Citizens|Concerned Citizens Committee]] under Calvin Cyrus as a means to keep youngsters out of trouble. It was a resounding success and now people of all ages and backgrounds volunteer here.
- The locals here are enamoured with the community garden and everything it represents. They are delighted to come here and work in nature for the benefit of their community. This place fills them with hope for the future and a sense of serenity
- The stores nearby are selling more and more of the local produce and the residents of the neighborhood are rapidly adopting the fad and consuming less processed foods. This is gradually turning Whitecliff into an intentional community and a self-sustained neighborhood.
- Based on the style of clothing, tools, and architecture, historians and archaeologists can connect this place with Albion, the ancient Isle of Britain (or at least how it is portrayed in medieval legends).
- Beyond the Mist, the place looks like an ancient or medieval farmland. Nearby clusters of buildings look like little hamlets but where the concrete jungle grows dense, it takes the form of a dark forest. Those who explore that area may reach the [[overgrown ruin]].
- Beyond the Mist, the Enclave can be linked with the same Rift that Touched the Errants, but the source itself is hidden in writhing mist (Cyrus/Beaumont).
- The farmers are supportive of the Errants, claiming the gang is fighting the other gangs and Mayor Ricci to make more room for their garden and to embetter the life of the community.
- Every night, when there are fewer people around, one of the Errants’ bikers shows up to deliver a bag of magical seeds and pick up some provisions. Sometimes they recruit some of the farmers to join their cause. They can be followed to [[the basketball court]], [[the junkyard]], or [[the highway barricades]].
