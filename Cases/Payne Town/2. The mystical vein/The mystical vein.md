# Overview
The alchemist ARMAND KAI (Fulcanelli, a fabled master alchemist whose true identity is unknown) came to Corbin Payne seeking the Grail and everlasting life. Instead, the Fisher King revealed to him the king’s own festering wound, laying bare and amplifying Armand’s fear of aging and of death. The Fisher King then gave Armand a drop of the liquid that was in the Grail, containing a fraction of the Grail’s miraculous powers, and sent the alchemist to find a cure for his wound.

Whether to save himself from the burden of old age or to cure Corbin Payne, Armand Kai began to frantically seek the Philosopher’s Stone, or the Elixir of Life, an alchemical means to immortality. He confined himself to [[CANSELIET MANOR]], his expansive walledoff estate on the edge of the Old Quarter, where he conducted strange experiments. Decades went by and he grew older, although he was able to slow the process of aging using the powers of the Grail Water. But time was pressing. Just when Armand was about to despair, he unlocked the secret of the Grail Water: it had the power to awaken Sleepers to their Mythos.

This was the key: by awakening individuals to their Mythos, Armand could create a legendary person, creature, or substance that could impart longevity, perhaps even immortality. To provide him with fresh candidates for experimentation, he founded a false cult, [[MAGNUM OPUS]] (“the Great Work” of the Alchemists), selling an alchemical path to enlightenment while in truth using the naive recruits as subjects for awakening.

Within Magnum Opus, the naive recruits destined for awakening are called “Lambs”. The more unscrupulous and cutthroat recruits, the “Wolves”, are spared the forced awakening and are allowed to climb up the ranks of the cult to become Armand’s [[DISCIPLES]], whom he teaches basic transmutations of alchemical elements.

Once awakened, Lambs are cast into the fires of [[THE GLASSWORKS FURNACE]], which can strip a Rift from their Mythos powers (an invention of Armand with the Rift of the Gingerbread Witch). The unrefined product of the process, a thick liquid called [[ICHOR]], contains the essence of the Rift’s former Mythos powers, which Armand can transform into a [[Tinctures|TINCTURE]]. Anyone who holds the tincture of a Mythos can control its powers as if they were its Rift.

Armand arms his strongest allies with useful tinctures. When the Rift of the Gingerbread Witch was herself cast into the furnace by the Rift of Gretel, [[Rachel Woods]], Armand gave Woods the tincture of her predecessor’s powers and charged her with operating the furnace. A self-promoting crook, Woods started marketing a new drug called [[Candy]] to lure Rifts to her furnace, add to Armand’s victims, and make more Ichor for him and money for herself.

Armand is searching specifically for tinctures that can point him at the Philosopher’s Stone and life everlasting. He conducts his experiments at his lab in the depths of his manor, now a complex built to take visitors (and interlopers) through a process of alchemical decomposition and reconstruction. The manor also serves as a base of operations for Magnum Opus’ most advanced Disciples. Armand intends to continue to deceive more and more innocent seekers, forcefully awaken them, and then burn them in the furnace until he finds what he seeks.

# The Mystical Vein as the Crew's Origin Story
You can use Armand Kai’s operation, which is in the business of creating Rifts, as an origin story for some of your crew members or all of them. Crew members may have been awakened by Magnum Opus, in which case all they remember is joining a cult or being abducted, being anointed by hooded figures in a cavernous hall while tied to a stone altar, and eventually escaping. If your group wants to enhance the mystery, you may decide that they don’t know what happened to them at all, and that due to the proximity to their awakening, their memories are blurred or blocked out altogether.

You can also play through the crew’s awakening scene, either at the beginning of the story arc or in the middle, as a flashback, when someone recalls it. Set the scene at the Catacombs (see the case Cult of the God-King, page 146) and give the players a brief recap of what happened to their characters so far. Let them play as their Sleepers versions, without using tags from Mythos themes, improvements, or Moments of Evolution. Once they are anointed by the Magnum Opus cultist monks, each character can use her basic Mythos powers. (The number of Mythos themes doesn’t matter here; technically, the PC may have been awakened and instantly became a Legendary. Decide which powers are at her disposal based on the narrative.) You can even decide that everyone awakening has just one nascent theme (see Player’s Guide, Playing the Awakening, page 65, and Nascent Themes, page 265).

# Themes an motifs
- **Mystical Shenanigans**: The Mystical Vein explores the section of the Rift underworld that takes special interest in the occult and the supernatural. It combines the mystery of the magical arts with a modern setting: hooded cultists meet in abandoned candle-lit subway tunnels, ancient scripts glow bright when read aloud, and mystics in business suits vie for control over coveted artifacts. The cases in this vein are written in the spirit of the urban fantasy genre.
- **Alchemical Process**: Everything in Armand Kai’s operation is built around an ancient alchemical process and its four stages: blackening (rotting), whitening (purification), yellowing (awakening), and reddening (transformation). On the surface, this alchemical process refers to the transmutation of lead into gold; in its deeper meaning, it refers to the transformation of the human soul, the possible transition from Sleeper to Rift, and the discovery of one’s true nature.
- **Self-Preservation and Greed**: Armand Kai’s morbid fear of dying drives him to rob and hoard other human beings’ Mythoi (or souls) as if they were a resource to be consumed, and those in his service are no different. This vein features self-promoting disciples, hired-guns, and underworld hustlers who harden their hearts in the face of others’ suffering so that they can survive.
- **Seeking a Higher Truth**: Conversely, many of the NPCs in this vein are in search for secret knowledge or a mystical experience that will bring about a transformation, either within them or in the world around them (some are also the aforementioned greedy servants of Armand Kai).
