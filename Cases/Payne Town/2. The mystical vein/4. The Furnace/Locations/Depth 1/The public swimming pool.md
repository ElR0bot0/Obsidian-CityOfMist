# Background
The public swimming pool is a backdrop location where the crew can investigate the case of Juan Delamar, the City-renowned gold medalist swimmer who lost his talent overnight.

The person to talk to is Coach Bradley Ferrell (Sleeper), Juan’s swimming coach, who is concerned about his failing performance in the last week. The coach would like to get professional help for Juan but he’s **wary-of-bad-publicity-1**; if the crew approaches him without an invitation or some credentials (as police, a PI, a counselor, etc.), increase this status to tier 3.

Another option is hitting the locker room after practice and chatting to Juan’s teammates. They know more about Juan’s personal (and secret) life, but are **protecting-their-champ-2**, although a more talkative kid, perhaps someone jealous of Juan’s success or someone deeply concerned for him, could be picked out with a social Investigate move, circumventing the status.
# Clues
- Juan has been a prodigious swimmer throughout his teens (ruling out a connection between his swimming and Candy).
- Since he went on “that wild weekend” when he didn’t come home, his performance crashed: he seemed to have lost every ounce of swimming talent he once had. Where once he would gracefully cut through the water, he now seemed comparatively clumsy, swimming with good technique but lacking his former extraordinary strength and competence.
- The Coach worked hard to keep this indiscretion from the team’s administrative staff, fearing Juan would be expelled from the team. He is very eager to get Juan back in his game.
- Juan used Candy to find love. Being of homely appearance and suffering from insecurities, Juan had trouble initiating romantic relationships with girls. A few weeks ago he started dating a girl from his college, but she left him after that fateful weekend.
- One of his teammates knows Juan has been going out to the Industrial Zone ([[The Bowl fight club]]) since he was seventeen. He also picked up an empty Candy wrapper that Juan once dropped.
- Juan got his Candy from Carter ([[The Bowl fight club]]).
At some point, Juan himself may show up for practice (or the crew may find him at his family home). He is a lean, dark-haired young man of below-average height, about twenty years of age, carrying a duffle bag with swim gear. He is quiet, calm, and keeps to himself.
- During practice, Juan seems to go through the motions mechanically. Other swimmers as well as Coach Farrell stare at him and whisper, but this does not elicit any reaction from Juan, not even a shrug or a clenched jaw as one would expect. He simply seems numb.
- Juan doesn’t remember the details of his Candy addiction and weekend bender. All he was told when he woke up is that he dabbled with the wrong drugs and had a bad trip, and that it could cost him his swimming career. People around him berate him for squandering his talent, but he doesn’t really care. He is seriously considering quitting swimming.
- While he still looks quite plain, Juan no longer displays some of his Mythos’s facial features as seen on a past team photo: a pug nose, bony protrusions, and bulging eyes.
- **Beyond the Mist**, Juan is a Sleeper. Further beyond the Mist, he carries a trace of the furnace Mythos (see The Furnace Trace sidebar, page 93)
