# Background
The Gingerbread House Enclave is the heart of the Candy operation and the abode of the new Gingerbread Witch, Rachel Woods. This is both where Candy is manufactured and where Candy leads: the furnace, and the end of one’s Mythos.

The Enclave is a mirror image of the factory it resides in, but it is superimposed with the Mythos of the Gingerbread House (a house made of sweets). The walls, posts, beams, doors, and windows of the Enclave are a strange mixture of industrial and cabin construction; upon closer inspection, they are revealed to be made of cookies, chocolate, and glazed sugar – and suffused with Candy. Cabin furniture and the flickering firelight also hint at the Mythos of this place. As with the factory, the furnace is set at the center of the Enclave, although here it is active and shaped more like a fairytale stove. There are vats of pure Candy stored in the back of the Enclave, close to a large cauldron, where Rachel concocts the drug. Anyone foolish enough to try to consume Candy within the Enclave gets **caged-4** instantly (without Facing Danger), but no wish is fulfilled.
## The cages
Candy users who enter the Enclave are soon engulfed by psychedelic iridescent lights which form a cage around them (**caged-4**, without Facing Danger). Only they and other caged individuals can see its luminous frame. The cage limits the mobility of the person inside it like a material cage and its scintillating energy counters the use of Mythos powers. Any action to break out of the cage or affect anything outside it from inside is affected by this status. Attempts to reduce this status from the outside are not affected by the status (assuming the rescuer isn’t caged herself) but require arcane know-how to undo its magic as the cage has no lock and its “bars” are made of shifting energy. It is possible to reduce the status partially, but the status must be fully removed to free a character from the cage.

The bearer of the Gingerbread Witch Tincture – currently Rachel Woods – knows intuitively when a cage was triggered. She also controls the cages and can manipulate them around the factory space (as if she is telekinetically moving her captives), though it requires gesturing or snapping her fingers. Captives can resist being moved by holding onto something through the “bars” of the cage, but such actions are hindered by the **caged** status.

Rachel uses the cages to force her captives into the furnace. Once she has made the soft move to direct a cage into the furnace, the PCs may make one or two moves before the cage and the captive enter the burning furnace, and optionally another move before the furnace door shuts. Forcing a cage or someone inside to move against the instruction of the Gingerbread Witch incurs the **caged** status.
## Inside the furnace
The shared invention of Armand Kai and the original Gingerbread Witch, the furnace “cooks” Rifts so their Mythos essence can be “sucked out” of them. The furnace container is made of industrial-grade materials and is difficult to break (break:5 and reduce all physical harm statuses by 2). The flames of the furnace are cool and do not harm Sleepers or non-Relic inanimate objects. As with the cages, the furnace responds to gesture commands from the bearer of the Gingerbread Witch Tincture.

When a Rift is inside the burning furnace, they take a **Mythos-burning-4** status periodically. During this painful process, they feel their essence leaving them while sparks, smoke, and other effects related to their Mythos explode all around them. Depending on your cinematic considerations and the challenge level you want your crew to face, this status can be given either as a hard move (slower cook, allowing more opportunities to escape) or as a soft move (rapid cook, creating extreme pressure and possibly dire consequences). At tier-6, the Rift becomes a **Mythos-drained-Sleeper-6**.

Soon thereafter, Ichor starts dripping heavily out of a pipe at the back of the furnace, glimmering with the Mythos’ colors and radiating feelings, ideas, and psychic images of the story of the Mythos. At this point, Ichor is stable and dormant and its Mythos energy cannot be easily activated; however, it is an extremely corrosive substance

If an attempt to interrupt the process fails, especially if this occurs while the Rift tries to Stop. Holding. Back., and the **Mythos-burning** reaches tier 6, she may transform into a Gingerbread Man instead of a Sleeper. The Ichor from Gingerbread Men is contaminated and unusable, its Mythos dissipated; no one studied yet where these poor souls’ Mythos transfers in such a case.
## The gingerbread men
Scattered around the dark recesses of the Enclave are some twenty or so of Rachel’s victims whose “cooking” went wrong, the Gingerbread Men: burnt, gnarled, rust-colored wights, oozing neutral white Ichor. Their intellect damaged and their body infused with the furnace Mythos, the Gingerbread Men respond only to simple commands from the Gingerbread Witch. Rachel uses them as a second line of defense in case unwanted guests invade the Enclave or a Rift somehow breaks loose. When she calls them, these zombie-like creatures emerge from the shadows and do her bidding. Anyone Sneaking Around the Enclave may also bump into them inadvertently.
## Facing rachel woods
When the PCs reach the furnace, Rachel Woods is there, working. She could be concocting more Candy, but ideally, she is about to cook one of the PCs, a regular member of the supporting cast, or a beloved NPC.

Rachel is a petite woman, rocking a goth leather and black lace outfit (a taste in fashion she picked up from the Gingerbread Witch Tincture). Feeling herself all-powerful and in control, she walks around the furnace swinging a baseball bat and chomping on (regular) candy. She is confident and sassy, as befitting her go-getter personality.

There’s a story behind Rachel’s ambition. Not unlike her alchemist boss, whom she never met, Rachel Woods is running from something, in this case her past as an orphan living in poverty. The drive to survive that helped her through a rough childhood on the streets still guides her and she is convinced that she does what she does for her survival and the survival of her last remaining family member, Hans. She is determined to make enough money from the Ichor business to never again have to live in poverty, and to do so, she had to not only seize the opportunity that fell in her lap but also harden her heart to the suffering of her victims. She tells herself that turning Rifts into Sleepers may even be better for them and that “that’s what they get for the fun they had” fulfilling their wishes. Little by little, she has turned as dark and as twisted as the Gingerbread Witch herself.

Rachel is here to make Ichor. It makes little difference to her whether the crew tries to save themselves and other Rifts, sabotage the furnace, bring her down, or change her mind; anyone who gets in her way will end up cooked or dead. The one exception to this is Hans, whom she will not hurt (as per True Love), but not hurting Hans doesn’t necessarily mean doing what he wants. If Hans gets in the way, Rachel may restrain or subdue him to get the job done.
### Clues
- Streetwise, savvy, or sensitive PCs could figure out Rachel’s ambition and past trauma. Mind readers may be hindered by her move Defense Against Magic.
- In the gallery, under Rachel’s bed, there are six duffle bags full of money, her reward for the Ichor she harvested for Magnum Opus.
- Rachel doesn’t know the people who collect the Ichor or their “Master”, but it is obvious that their main concern was collecting more and more Mythoi. Trying to ambush the Ichor collecting truck leads to the Magnum Opus Shipment.
## [[The gingerbread witch Tincture]]
Hanging on a chain from Rachel’s neck is a tiny cylindrical vial no larger than a finger, containing a syrupy caramel-colored liquid: the Gingerbread Witch Tincture. This is the source of all her Gingerbread Witch powers. Rachel also retains her own Rift powers thanks to the connection between the two Mythoi and Armand attuning the Tincture to her specifically.

The following effects are created and sustained by the Gingerbread Witch Tincture. Even if Rachel loses the Tincture in a struggle:
- **Candy** still causes Rifts entering the Enclave to be caged;
- **the cages** operate normally and continue moving to the destination they were last instructed;
- **the furnace** fire continues to burn or remains dead;
- **the Gingerbread Men** follow their last orders; and
- **the Enclave** remains accessible only to Candy users.
To stop or change these effects, a PC or NPC must seize the Tincture vial and successfully control its power. For a Rift PC, taking momentary control of the Tincture requires a Stop. Holding. Back. move with a significant sacrifice and allows the PC to issue a handful of commands to the Gingerbread Witch creations in the above list before the connection breaks. PCs with arcane knowledge may use it to Go Toe to Toe with the Gingerbread Mythos in the Tincture for the same effect.

Attempting to take permanent control of the Tincture can have a negative, neutral, or positive outcome depending on how her own Mythos interacts with the Gingerbread Witch Mythos. Read more about activating Tinctures on page 86.
### Clues
- Studying the Gingerbread Witch Tincture shows it was made of refined Ichor, extracted from Rosina Leckermaul.
- The Gingerbread Witch Tincture can be studied to learn more about Tinctures in general. It may also point to Armand’s manor (see The Alchemist, page 155) but will more likely draw an interception attempt by Magnum Opus Disciples or the Yakuza Yokai (see Cult of the God-King, page 120).
